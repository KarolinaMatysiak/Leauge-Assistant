League of Friends was created as a passion project to enhance the gaming experience for the author's friends and make champion selection in League of Legends easier. It was designed for players who want a simple way to browse available champions, filter them by various criteria, and randomly select one for their game. Although League of Friends was initially a dedication to the author’s friends, the website is now available to all players looking for a convenient tool to manage their champion selection. The website features two main sections. Champion List allows users to view the full list of champions available in the game. This section includes a filtering system that helps users quickly find champions based on specific criteria, such as role or other attributes, making it easier to analyze available options and select the most suitable champion for a given game. The second section, Champion Roulette, is designed for random champion selection. Users can choose a preferred role, and the system will randomly select a champion that fits that position. This feature is particularly useful for players who are undecided about which champion to play or who want to introduce an element of randomness to their gameplay by trying out champions they wouldn’t normally pick. For logged-in users, an additional feature allows them to create custom folders with their favorite champions. This means they can randomize champion selection only from their chosen set, providing even more personalization and making the tool more tailored to individual preferences. The website also offers a user-friendly interface, ensuring smooth navigation and quick access to all features. Whether players are looking for a strategic pick or simply want to experiment with new champions, League of Friends provides a hassle-free way to explore different options. League of Friends is not just a tool but a passion project built with the community in mind. It stands as a testament to the enjoyment of playing together, making champion selection more engaging and accessible for everyone.
